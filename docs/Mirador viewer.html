The Reconstruction of the Otho-Corpus Gospels

This page serves to simultaneous present the two main codices in which the text of the Otho-Gospels can be found today - British Library Cotton MS Otho C V (at left) and Corpus Christi College, Cambridge, MS 197b (at right) - flanking a recombined Gospel Book that serves to approximately reconstruct the manuscript before it was taken apart. The reconstructed codex was created using the IIIF manifests from both institutions and the Bodleian's IIIF Manifest Editor, available at https://digital.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/manifest-editor. In the combined digital object, folio and page numbering has been preserved from each institution to facilite cross-collection comparison, however, a table of contents has been created in order to help the user navigate through the four Gospels in their canonical order. A discussion of these two manuscripts and the digital reconstruction was featured in the BL's Medieval Manuscripts blog here and on the Parker Library Blog here.
